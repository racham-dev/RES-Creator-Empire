## Part 2: AI Models – The Brain (15 Minutes)

*(Screen share a simple slide or diagram.)*

**Script:**

> *"So, what is an AI Model?*
>
> *Think of an AI model as a **human brain** that has been trained to do one specific thing really well.*
>
> *- Some models are trained to recognize images.*
> *- Some models are trained to understand language.*
> *- Some models are trained to generate music.*
>
> *Here is the most important thing to understand: There are many different AI models, and each one has a specific strength.*
>
> **Large Language Models (LLMs)**
> - *Examples: GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini.*
> - *Best for: Writing, brainstorming, coding, chatting.*
>
> **Image Models**
> - *Examples: Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, Leonardo’s Phoenix Model.*
> - *Best for: Creating visuals, paintings, product designs.*
>
> **Video Models**
> - *Examples: Runway Gen-2, Pika Labs.*
> - *Best for: Generating short video clips and animations.*
>
> **Audio Models**
> - *Examples: Suno AI, Udio, ElevenLabs.*
> - *Best for: Generating music, voiceovers, sound effects.*
>
> *Here is the golden rule: The better you understand a model's strength, the better you can use it. You don't use a video model to write a novel. And you don't use a language model to generate a 3D image.*
>
> *When you master different models, you become a **multimedia AI director**."*

## Part 3: AI Agents – The Assistant (15 Minutes)

*(Screen share a demonstration of an AI Agent setup, e.g., ChatGPT Tasks or a Make.com automation.)*

**Script:**

> *"Now, let's talk about **AI Agents**.*
>
> *An AI Model is a brain. But an AI Agent is a **worker**. It is a system that uses a model to **do a task for you**, often autonomously.*
>
> *Think of it like this:*
> *- **Model:** A chef who knows how to cook every dish.*
> *- **Agent:** A waiter who goes to the kitchen, gets the chef to cook, and brings you the food.*
>
> *In the real world, an AI Agent can:*
> *- Write a blog post and automatically post it to your website.*
> *- Check your emails and draft replies for you.*
> *- Monitor a competitor’s social media and alert you when they post.*
>
> *You don't need to code to use agents. Tools like **Make.com (formerly Integromat)** and **Zapier** let you build agents without writing a single line of code.*
>
> *Let me show you a simple example:*
>
> *(Open Make.com or Zapier.)*
>
> *"I will create an agent that does this:*
> *1. When I add a new row to my Google Sheets database.*
> *2. The AI writes a personalized welcome email.*
> *3. The agent sends that email automatically.*
>
> *Do you see that? The AI didn't just think. It **acted**. That is an agent."*

## Part 4: AI Gems – The Custom Expert (10 Minutes)

*(Screen share Google Gemini’s "Gems" interface or a custom GPT builder.)*

**Script:**

> *"Now, let's talk about the most exciting concept for business owners and creators: **AI Gems** (sometimes called Custom GPTs).*
>
> *A Gem is a **customized version of an AI model** that you train to be an expert in one specific area.*
>
> *Instead of using generic ChatGPT, you can create a Gem that specializes in exactly what you need.*
>
> *For example:*
> *- **The Nigerian Copywriter Gem:** You give it all your past successful ad copies. It learns your style. Now, when you ask it to write an ad, it sounds exactly like you.*
> *- **The Social Media Content Gem:** You tell it your brand tone, your audience, and your hashtags. Every time you ask it for a post, it gives you a ready-to-post caption.*
> *- **The Voiceover Prompt Gem:** You give it the script structure for podcast intros. Every time you need a podcast script, it writes one instantly.*
>
> *In Google Gemini, these are called **Gems**. In ChatGPT, they are called **Custom GPTs**. In Claude, they are called **Projects**.*
>
> *The beauty of Gems is that they save you **hours of repetition**. You don't have to explain your business every time you open the AI. The Gem already knows."*
